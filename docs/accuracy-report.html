The one thing that matters

Your math is already excellent. Heading is the bottleneck.

The positional pipeline is rigorous — full WGS84 ECEF→ENU transforms, atmospheric refraction, lunar parallax, sidereal star math. All of it is sub-degree accurate.

But the entire virtual sky is rotated to true north by one number from the compass, and that number is wrong by 5–25°. A 0.05° geometry error is invisible; a 10° heading error puts every plane a hand's-width from where it really is.

≈ 90% of perceived inaccuracy is device pose, not math.

Research finding: smartphone magnetometer+gyro fusion tops out around 17.4° RMS in the field and only ~4–5° even under ideal, anomaly-free conditions — so sub-degree heading is physically unattainable from the compass. ARKit's .gravityAndHeading won't help either; Apple derives its north from the same CLHeading. Precise apps (PeakFinder) align to a known object (a mountain silhouette) instead. Conclusion: plate-solving against the sun/moon/known aircraft is the only route to the accuracy users expect.

Prioritized plan

P0 · POSE

Plate-solve heading against known objects

Demote the compass to a coarse ±15° bootstrap. Continuously, automatically solve the heading offset from the sun/moon (azimuth known to ~0.01°) whenever one is above ~3° — extend to bright planets/stars. Promote the existing lockToSun()/lockToMoon() from a one-shot manual lock to an always-on correction.

Expected: ~10–20° → <0.5° in clear conditions
P0 · POSE

One-tap "snap to this plane" (all-weather solve)

ADS-B identity is exact, so a tapped aircraft's true bearing is known. The cloudy-day analog of PeakFinder's landmark solve — one tap aligns the whole sky. Builds on the existing pan-to-align (SkylightAR.swift:1881).

Expected: instant exact fix, no compass needed
POSE

Fuse ARKit yaw + compass; show the residual

1-D complementary/Kalman filter: ARKit yaw (smooth, drifts) + plate-solve (absolute truth) + compass (coarse fallback). Replace the open-loop proportional steerer (alignNorth(), :1741) and always surface the measured error vs. the sun/moon so calibration mistakes are visible, not silent.

Expected: kills SLAM drift + the ~5 s lag to correct bias
P1 · DATA

Fix aircraft altitude datum & extrapolation

Prefer geometric (WGS84) altitude; QNH-correct barometric (it assumes 1013 hPa). Use the observer's ellipsoidalAltitude (WGS84) — not .altitude (geoid/MSL) — so both ends of the range vector share a datum. Dead-reckon altitude via vertical rate, not just lat/lon.

Expected: removes tens of metres of geoid + datum error; fixes climb/descent
P1 · DATA

Per-source latency + auto-refresh ISS TLE

The fixed feedLatencySec = 1.5 is wrong for FR24's 8 s polling — set it per source and prefer the 1 Hz feed. Refresh the ISS TLE daily (today: once per session, never refreshed, :1631); never use one >10 days old.

Expected: less fast-mover lag; ISS error <1 km
P2 · POLISH

Refraction P/T, best-accuracy GPS, time sync

Scale Bennett refraction by (P/1010)×(283/(273+T)) from METAR. Request kCLLocationAccuracyBest in AR. Add a one-shot SNTP offset at launch — a 1 s clock error ≈ 7.7 km of ISS position.

Expected: sub-0.1° near-horizon + drift insurance

Suggested sequence

Instrument first. Accuracy HUD + a single regression metric (rendered vs. confirmed sun/moon error) — so every change is measurable.
Plate-solving (P0 #1 & #2). Celestial auto-align + tap-to-snap — fastest, most visible accuracy jump.
Sensor fusion (P0 #3). ARKit↔compass complementary filter — the durable structural fix.
Data layer (P1). Altitude datum + extrapolation, per-source latency, daily TLE refresh.
Polish (P2). Refraction P/T, best-accuracy GPS, SNTP time sync.

Verification — refuted during research (not facts)

  • Magnetometer EKF fusion reaching sub-degree under ideal conditions — refuted (best ≈ 4–5°).
  • SPA refraction model accurate to ±0.0003° — refuted as overstated.
  • TLE error growing as age1.5 — not confirmed (treat as ~linear).
  • ARKit eulerAngles.y == 0 exactly at true north under gravityAndHeading — refuted.
