# Accessibility — Hear & Feel the Sky

Overhead is an augmented-reality sky app — but it does not require sight to
use. **"Hear & feel the sky"** lets a blind or low-vision user locate a real
aircraft overhead using spatial sound and touch alone. We believe it is the
only plane-spotting app you can use with your eyes closed.

Toggle it on in **Profile → Accessibility → Hear & feel the sky.**

## What it does

- **Hear it.** Every nearby aircraft becomes a 3D-positioned engine hum,
  rendered with HRTF spatial audio (best with AirPods). A plane to your left
  sounds to your left; one climbing overhead rises above you. Point toward the
  sound and you are facing the plane.
- **Feel it.** As a plane nears the center of where you are pointing, the phone
  pulses — slow and soft when it is off to the side, accelerating to fast and
  firm when you are aimed straight at it. It is a Geiger-counter for aircraft:
  sweep the sky and feel for the plane. No screen, no glance required.
- **Together**, sound gives you the direction and touch confirms the lock — a
  complete, eyes-free way to find what is flying over you.

## Built on the platform, for everyone

- **VoiceOver**: interface controls are labeled and navigable.
- **Reduce Motion**: animated rings and pulses are stilled when the system
  setting is on.
- **Dynamic Type**: text scales with the reader's preferred size.
- **No precision required**: large, forgiving tap targets; the haptic and audio
  guidance do not depend on hitting a tiny on-screen glyph.

## Why it matters (App Store selling point)

Most AR apps are, by definition, for people who can see the screen. Overhead
turns the same live data — real aircraft positions, the Sun, the Moon, the
ISS — into an experience you can **hear and feel**. It is inclusive by design,
not as an afterthought: a sighted child and a blind grandparent can stand in
the same backyard and both find the plane overhead.

This is the headline of our inclusivity story, and the reason Overhead belongs
in the conversation for design and accessibility recognition.
