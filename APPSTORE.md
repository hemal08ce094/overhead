## Description
Hold your iPhone up and the sky explains itself.

Every aircraft overhead appears in augmented reality at its true position —
airline, type, destination, even a photo of the exact airframe. The sun, the
moon at tonight's phase, the visible planets, 1,600 stars and their
constellations, and the International Space Station share the same sky, all
placed by on-device astronomy that's accurate to a fraction of a degree.

Then Overhead does something no other app can: it predicts when a plane will
cross the face of the moon or sun from exactly where you stand — and counts
you down to the moment, shutter ready.

— Live aircraft with routes, photos, and honest data: when a flight is visibly
  landing somewhere other than its filed route says, Overhead tells you.
— Favorites and Focus: follow one flight with a find-it arrow, in the Dynamic
  Island, and on your lock screen.
— Sky sounds: every nearby plane becomes a 3D-positioned engine hum. Close
  your eyes and point at the sound. Best with AirPods.
— Sky events: solar eclipses computed for YOUR location (don't miss
  August 12), meteor showers, full moons — narrated by on-device intelligence.
— Ask Siri: "What's flying over me?"
— StandBy moon clock for your nightstand. Apple Watch shows your next ISS pass.
— Dark-sky mode: the full star map with the camera off, kind to your battery
  and your night vision.

No account. No tracking. Everything computes on your iPhone.

## App Review notes
Location is core functionality (the app positions aircraft and celestial
objects relative to the reviewer's coordinates, like a navigation app). If
location is declined, the app continues in a demo sky. All features are
testable indoors via Dark sky mode. Aircraft data comes from the public
airplanes.live feed; no login required.
